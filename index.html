📖 How to Read the Data:
Seed Colors = Advancement Probability: Gold (1-seed = 84% advance) → Silver (2-seed = 78%) → Bronze (3-seed = 68%) → Blue (4-5 seeds = 62/60%) → Orange (6-8 seeds = 54/48/44%) → Red (11-16 seeds = 15% or less). Colors don't mean "better" or "worse"—they're just visual markers for how often teams actually advance past the first round in tournament history.
Value Score Explained: We weight three factors: 35% PPG (how many points the player scores per game), 35% their seed's historical win rate, 30% tournament advancement odds. Higher value = player will score AND their team likely advances.
How We Got These Numbers: We looked at 20+ years of NCAA tournament results. 1-seeds make it past Round 1 about 84% of the time. 16-seeds? Almost never (1%). We averaged all the PPG stats from recent seasons and normalized everything to the highest scorer. This is just history—not a prediction—but history repeats in March.

Rounds 1-3: Chase elite 1-3 seeds. Higher advancement odds = predictable points. Value score heavily favors these.
Rounds 4-8: Balance act. A 6-seed scoring 20 PPG might beat a 4-seed scoring 16 PPG. Check PPG vs Seed tradeoff. Don't reach for seeds if PPG drops >3 points.
Rounds 9-16: Punt elite seeds. Stack volume scorers from 11-16 seeds (25+ PPG) = higher ceiling than mediocre 5-seeds. High variance, but mid-draft is where you build equity.
